# The "Private Banking" Model Strategy

This document maps the **Retail vs. Private Banking** concept to the three entities of the Moye Law ecosystem.

## 1. The Factory (Sushi Kitchen) = The "FinTech Core" (Retail)
*   **Role:** This is the high-tech infrastructure (the "ATM" or "Mobile App").
*   **Function:** It powers the **Retail Experience**. It delivers standardized, efficient, high-quality legal products (like simple incorporation or standard wills) at scale.
*   **User Experience:** "Cool Grey" & "Mono" aesthetic. Fast, transparent, fixed-price.
*   **Who uses it:** *Everyone*. The average client uses it for everything; the specific High-Net-Worth (HNW) client uses it for routine tasks because it's efficient.

## 2. The Storefront (Moye Law) = The "Bank Branch" (The Interface)
This is where the **Seamless Blend** happens. The website acts as the building itself.
*   **The Lobby (Public View):** Clean, modern, efficient. Showcases the "Sushi-powered" efficiency. "We are a modern law firm."
*   **The Private Wing (Hidden Layer):** The **Bespoke Counsel**.
    *   This is the "Deep Navy & Gold" experience.
    *   It isn't just a product on a shelf; it's a *relationship*.
    *   It "exposes itself as needed"—when the complexity of the client's problem exceeds the "Retail" solutions, the "Private Banker" (Christopher Moye) steps in.

## 3. The IP Holder (Sushi Legal) = The "Bank Charter"
*   **Role:** The entity that owns the *proprietary financial products*.
*   **Significance:** It separates the *tools* from the *advice*. Sushi Legal owns the "LegacyOS" recipe; Moye Law *licenses* it to serve clients. This structure protects the asset if Moye Law ever scales or spins off the bespoke practice.

## 💡 Design Implication for the Website
Instead of splitting every card 50/50 (which equals them), we could structure the site like a **Tiered Access** system:
1.  **Standard View:** "Tech-Forward Legal Solutions" (Sushi-backed). Trusted, Efficient.
2.  **The "Key" (Elevation):** A clear, distinct pathway for "Private Client Services." Maybe a "Concierge" mode or a specific section that feels gateless but elevated—like walking through a heavy oak door in a modern glass lobby.
