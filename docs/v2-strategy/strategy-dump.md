# Strategic Architecture: Moye Law "Kinetic Structuralism"

## Executive Summary
This document serves as the definitive blueprint for the Moye Law website redesign. It synthesizes the "Fifth Wave" legal strategy with a unique visual identity defined as "Kinetic Structuralism"—a fusion of Piet Mondrian’s rigid engineering and Saul Bass’s narrative tension.

## Part I: The "Kinetic Structuralist" Design System
The static grid is replaced by a living architecture. The website does not just "load"; it "constructs" itself around the content, using the scroll bar as a timeline of assembly.

### 1.1 The "Intersect & Reveal" Motion Logic
We are adopting an "Apple-Style" Scrollytelling mechanic. The grid lines are not decorative borders; they are active agents.
*   **The Mechanism:** As the user scrolls, vertical and horizontal lines travel across the screen (x/y translation).
*   **The Reveal:** Content (text, images) is initially hidden (Opacity 0). It only becomes visible when two grid lines intersect to close a rectangle. This mimics the "Door Effect" from Saul Bass’s title sequences.

### 1.2 The "Navy-Duotone" Imaging Protocol
To resolve the tension between "Abstract Graphics" (Bass) and "Human Trust" (Family Law), we will process photography to behave like graphics.
*   **The Technique:** Hero images and primary portraits will undergo a Duotone Mapping.
    *   Dark Tones -> Deep Navy (#0A2342)
    *   Light Tones -> Warm Gold (#C99D56) or Pure White (#FFFFFF)

### 1.3 The "Data Mesh" Visualization
We will introduce "High-Density" zones to showcase the "Sushi Kitchen" capabilities without breaking the aesthetic.
*   **Desktop:** A tight, spreadsheet-like sub-grid (The Mesh).
*   **Mobile Adaptation:** On screens <768px, the "Data Mesh" must transform into a "Card Carousel."

## Part II: The Quarantine Protocol (Strict Exclusions)
To prevent "Brand Contamination," the following elements are explicitly forbidden:
*   **NO "Sushi" Metaphors:** No "Omakase," "Menu," "Chef," or "Ingredients."
*   **NO "Salmon" or "Neon":** The color #FA8072 is banned.
*   **NO "Hacker" Aesthetics:** No terminal green on black, no glitch effects.
*   **NO "Ultra Light" Hairlines:** Borders must be 4px (Structural).

## Part III: The Fifth Wave and the Strategic Necessity
The industry is entering the Fifth Wave, driven by "Agentic Artificial Intelligence" and "Inverse Design."
*   **The Shift:** From "Artisan Billable Hour" to "Computed Agentic Outcome."
*   **Inverse Design:** The user defines the outcome (Minimize Liability <5%), and the agent optimizes the solution.

## Part IV: The "Factory to Storefront" Model
*   **The Factory ("Sushi Kitchen"):** Backend, binary, brutalist, powerful.
*   **The Storefront (Moye Law):** Frontend, family values, refined, trustworthy.
*   **The Strategy:** The website allows clients to buy the *output* of the factory without seeing the *machinery*.

## Part V: The "Paired Services" UX Architecture
Every practice area is presented as a Binary Choice:
*   **Left Cell (The Product):** "LegacyOS Bundle," "Fixed Price," "Deploy."
*   **Right Cell (The Service):** "Bespoke Counsel," "Hourly/Retainer," "Consult."

## Part VI: The Regulatory Seawall
The Fifth Wave clashes with Rule 5.4 (Ban on non-lawyer ownership). The "Regulatory Seawall" is the barrier preventing full automation. We must navigate UPL (Unauthorized Practice of Law) by keeping "Humans in the Loop" for the Storefront service.

## Part VII: Personas
*   **Alex (Tech Innovator):** Wants "Functional Minimalism" and "High-Velocity Maps."
*   **Barbara (Caregiver):** Wants "Safety" and "Clarity."
*   **David (Prudent Investor):** Wants "Authority" and "Structure."
